Projects

Graph-Based Analysis for ALS Patient Stratification

Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS) is a complex neurodegenerative disease characterized by diverse genetic and clinical heterogeneity, posing significant challenges to patient stratification and therapeutic intervention. Despite extensive genomic research, understanding the molecular mechanisms underlying ALS progression remain unknown. Leveraging comprehensive AnswerALS dataset, this study integrated transcriptomics and clinical data to explore ALS subgroups and identify genetic determinants of disease progression. By constructing graph-based patient representations that integrate prior biological knowledge, including Gene Ontology (GO) processes and KEGG pathways, and leveraging machine learning methods, we predict the slope of disease progression.

Semester Report, View Project

The Application of Machine Learning in Schottky Spectra Analysis

Schottky Spectra, that is random fluctuations of the macroscopic beam properties, such as beam position or intensity, contain rich information on machine parameters crucial for preserving the beam stability. Historically, the Schottky noise signals have been a powerful diagnostics tool in many accelerators, but in the LHC the Schottky-based diagnostics is difficult due to the presence of strong coherent components in the measured spectra. The goal of this project was to analyse and interpret high-dimensional data of the LHC Schottky spectra using machine learning techniques

Communes - Neuronal Sleep

In literature, physiological deep sleep is associated with a slow oscillating pattern of brain activity and the absence of physical activity, while waking behaviour is associated with continuous brain activity. The steadfast belief in clearly separable, global, stationary brain states is weakening. Recent studies suggest that sleep and wakefulness are intricate, dynamic and spatially heterogeneous phenomena. This work investigates the phenomena of local and global sleep in awake mice, analysing and characterising the spatio-temporal dynamics of these brief periods of sporadic neuronal firing, which we refer to as communes.

Intra-retinal Layer Segmentation of Optical Coherent Tomography Images using Deep Learning

The field of ophthalmology supplies neuroscientists with images of neural tissue through optical coherence tomography imaging. This imaging technique enables to capture images of the human retina, which is essentially an extension of the brain, making it highly suitable for obtaining medical information on neurological conditions. Such data provides both neuroscientists and researchers developing computer-aided systems with a new set of tools. This Bachelor's thesis focuses on researching, developing, and implementing models used for segmenting neurological structure in the images of optical coherence tomography.

GitHub, Bachelor's Thesis

Genetically Modified Wolf Optimization with SGD for Optimising DNN

When training Convolutional Neural Networks there is a large emphasis on creating efficient optimization algorithms and highly accurate networks. The state-of-the-art method of optimizing the networks is done by using gradient descent algorithms, such as Stochastic Gradient Descent. However, there are some limitations presented when using gradient descent methods. The major drawback is the lack of exploration, and over-reliance on exploitation. Hence, this research aims to analyze an alternative approach to optimizing neural network weights, with the use of population-based metaheuristic algorithms. A hybrid between Grey Wolf Optimizer and Genetic Algorithms is explored, in conjunction with SGD.
