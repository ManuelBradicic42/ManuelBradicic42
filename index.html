About Me

I am a computer scientist specialising in artificial intelligence and machine learning. I am currently pursuing an MSc in Machine Learning through a joint program at KTH Royal Institute of Technology (Sweden) and École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL, Switzerland).

Previously, I worked at CERN in the Beam Instrumentation Group, where I applied machine learning to beam diagnostics, contributing to research on Schottky spectra analysis. I also conducted research as a computational neuroscience intern at Karolinska Institutet, where I worked on analysing brain signals obtained by electrodes. Later, I joined the LTS4 group at EPFL and LuisierLab at Idiap Research Institute, focusing on AI applications in healthcare and life sciences.

I hold a BSc (Hons) in Computer Science from the University of Surrey, the UK, where I specialised in AI and conducted research on early diagnosis of neurodegenerative diseases by segmenting neurological layers in human retinal images.

Additionally, I gained industry experience at Sky UK as an Associate Development Engineer and at Ericsson Sweden as an R&D Engineer.
